Background facts of case: 
- Mr Liang claims that a concrete debris fell from a ceiling beam in room 2, hitting his head while he was making tea.
- The strike caused him to lose consciousness for a short period and fall to the ground.
- Some parts of the debris flew into his eyes.
- Mr Liang also claimed to have sustained lower back contusion, head injury, and right eye injury.
- The defendant's position is that they did not receive any complaint from Mr Liang about the concrete debris fall until 31 August 2017.
- Mr Liang maintains that the concrete debris fell on his head and back at the same time, while the original pleading stated that it fell on his head first and then his body.
- Mr Liang clarified in his answer to further and better particulars that the positions were head first and then the back.
- Mr Chan, the defendant's counsel, argues that the pleaded facts contrast with Mr Liang's testimony at trial, where he described that the concrete debris fell on his head and back at the same time.
- Mr Liang considered the pleadings to be correct because the two hits were almost concurrent.
- Mr Liang testified that the accident was sudden and sounded like thunder, causing him to lose consciousness and wake up in a lateral position with pain in his waist.
- Mr Liang's testimony is corroborated by other evidence, such as a record from Mr Cheung.
- The defendant's position is that they did not admit to Mr Liang's claims and only sent a contractor to conduct repairs after receiving the complaint on 31 August 2017.
- The photo taken by Mr Liang on 1 September 2017 depicts three fragments of a concrete slab, which Mr Hau, the contractor, recognized as similar to the one he removed from the ceiling on that day.


Legal Issues: 
- The legal issues discussed include the way the injuries were inflicted, the extent of Mr. Liang's injuries, and the timing of Mr. Liang's complaint about the concrete debris fall.
- The judge and the lawyers discussed these issues to determine the credibility of Mr. Liang's testimony and the validity of his claims.
- The judge ruled that Mr. Liang's testimony regarding the concrete debris falling on his head and back at the same time was credible, despite the discrepancy with the original pleading.
- The judge also ruled that Mr. Liang sustained lower back contusion, head injury, and right eye injury, as confirmed by orthopaedic experts.
- The judge did not go into the details of Mr. Liang's residual disabilities, as it was deemed unnecessary for the trial.
- The judge ordered the usual interest rates for general damages and pre-trial loss of earnings and special damages.
- The judge ruled that the costs of the proceedings would be awarded to the plaintiff, to be taxed if not agreed upon, and the plaintiff's own costs would be taxed in accordance with the Legal Aid Regulations.


Rulings: 
The judge made several rulings in the case. Firstly, the judge ordered the defendants to pay the plaintiff for loss of earning capacity, medical expenses, traveling expenses, and tonic food. The judge also ordered the defendants to pay interest on general damages from the date of the Writ to the date of judgment, as well as interest on pre-trial loss of earnings and special damages from the date of the accident to the date of judgment. Additionally, the judge made an order regarding costs, stating that the costs of the proceedings should be awarded to the plaintiff and should be taxed if not agreed upon, with the plaintiff's own costs to be taxed in accordance with the Legal Aid Regulations.
